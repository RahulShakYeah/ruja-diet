Your Plan at a Glance

Your gym classes are excellent for fat loss, mobility and functional strength — but alone they won't build lean muscle or get you to a 30–35 min 5K. The solution: replace 3 gym class days with PPL strength sessions (Mon/Wed/Fri), keep your best cardio classes (Tue/Thu), use Saturday for your long run, and Sunday for gentle yoga recovery. Every day = exactly 1 session. Your 1500 cal nutrition plan is calibrated perfectly to fuel this — expect ~0.4 kg fat loss/week alongside muscle gain and running fitness.

Why replace those 3 classes?

Monday's S&C, Wednesday's Plyometrics, and Friday's Pilates are all high-intensity, non-progressive sessions. PPL replaces them with structured progressive overload — the only way to build visible lean muscle and boost your resting metabolism. You keep Tue (KB/Vinyasa) as your cardio day and gain running sessions Thu & Sat. The result: more muscle, faster fat loss, and a 5K finish.

Progression Guide
How to Progress Each Week

Progressive overload is the key to results. Follow this simple rule:

📈
Add reps first
When you can complete all sets × reps with good form, add 2 reps before adding weight.
🏋️
Then add weight
Add 1–2 kg on upper body, 2–5 kg on lower body moves per week once reps are maxed.
📝
Track everything
Write weight & reps each session. You can't progress what you don't track.
😴
Muscle grows at rest
Sleep 7–8 hrs and eat your full 1500 cal — especially protein. Don't under-eat on training days.

Your Target Pace

To run 5K in 30–35 minutes, your goal pace is 6:00–7:00 min/km. In training, your easy runs should feel like 7:30–8:00 min/km (can talk in full sentences). Intervals and tempo runs push to 5:45–6:15 min/km. By Week 8 you will run the full 5K distance. By Week 11, you'll race it at target pace.

Why this plan works for you

At 27, 5'1", 64 kg your maintenance calories are ~1,750–1,900 kcal/day. This 1,500 cal plan creates a safe 300–400 cal daily deficit — enough for ~0.4 kg fat loss per week without starvation or muscle loss. The plan is high in protein (paneer, eggs, fish, chicken, sprouts) to preserve muscle while you're training hard for your 5K. Combined with your gym sessions, expect visible body composition changes within 4–6 weeks.

Key Nutrition Rules
🕐
Eat on time
Keep meal gaps to 3–4 hours. Skipping meals slows metabolism and increases cravings.
🧂
Cut hidden calories
No ghee, butter, sugar, fried foods, packaged juices. Max 2 tsp cooking oil/day (mustard or olive).
🍳
Cook smart
Grill, steam, sauté. Deep-frying doubles the calories. Air frying is fine as an alternative.
📊
Weigh weekly
Weight fluctuates ±1–2 kg daily (water, hormones). Weigh every Saturday morning, same time.
💪
Protein is non-negotiable
On training days, prioritise eggs, paneer, chicken, fish or tofu at every main meal.
🏃‍♀️
Fuel your run
On Thursday & Saturday run days, have snack 2 (egg or sprouts) 1.5 hrs before. Never run fully fasted for long runs.
